1The 30-second pitch

PPP gets a second app inside the same hub. The existing Command Center stays exactly as it is — same login, same screens, same routine. The New Platform lives next to it as a separate space for commercial projects.

In the New Platform, PPP runs everything from the moment a bid comes in to the moment the last invoice is paid: accounts, contacts, bids, estimates, proposals, contracts, projects, daily reports, change orders, invoices, payments, and closeout. Nothing is pulled from Salesforce — every record lives directly in this app, so edits land instantly and there's no syncing delay.

When an admin signs in at hub.precisionpaintingplus.net, they pick which app to open. Once inside, a small "Switch to ___" button at the bottom of the sidebar lets them hop between the two anytime. Anyone without access to both apps just goes straight to the one they have — no extra clicks.

3A typical commercial project, end to end

The walk-through of what a single job looks like inside the app, from the very first email to the final paid invoice.

1
A bid request comes in An owner or GC emails or submits a form. The app creates an "Opportunity" automatically. If the sender is someone PPP knows, it links to their Account. If not, it flags the opp for the estimator to link manually.
2
Estimator reviews plans, builds a number Plans, specs, and any RFP get uploaded to the opportunity. Estimator fills in the labor, materials, equipment, and subcontractor numbers. The app totals everything and shows margin.
3
Proposal goes out The estimator builds the proposal inside the app, hits send. PPP staff can see if/when the client opened it. Revisions get tracked (Rev 1, Rev 2…) so we always know what version was sent.
4
Bid is won — manually mark "Awarded" The estimator clicks "Awarded" on the opportunity. This is the step where Alex or Katie manually adds the project to the platform to start working on it. The app creates a project record from the awarded opportunity.
5
Project team gets assigned Multiple project managers can be added. Different people on different projects. A PM might be on three jobs, a super on two, a foreman on one. There's no limit — assign whoever needs to see this job.
6
Work starts — invoice as you go Field crew submits daily reports from their phones (works offline if no signal). The PM tracks labor, materials, equipment, subs, schedule, punch items. Office staff send invoices as work progresses — multiple invoices per job, each for a specific dollar amount, manually set whenever they choose.
7
Payments come in — mark each one When a check or wire arrives, office staff opens the invoice and marks it paid (full or partial). The amount and date are saved. The system tracks the running balance per invoice and per project.
8
Change orders happen — logged separately Owner adds scope mid-job. PM creates a change order, attaches the signed CO PDF, sets the dollar amount + schedule impact. Once approved, it adds to the project's revised contract value and shows up as a line on the next invoice.
9
Job wraps — closeout docs uploaded Punchlist gets closed out. Warranty paperwork, certificates, lessons learned. Retainage gets released as the final invoice. Project closes.

5The 9 sections — walked through one by one

The app is built in 9 sections that match the operating system diagram. Each section is a screen you'd click into.

1

Accounts (the companies PPP works with)

The companies PPP bids to or works for. Same company can have many projects over the years — the Account is the long-lived identity, projects flow off it.

📋 Accounts list
The full directory. Filter by rating, compliance status, or industry.
  • Company name + rating (A / B / C)
  • Lifetime revenue + number of active projects
  • When PPP last talked to them
  • "New Account" button top-right
🏢 Account detail — Info
  • Company name, DBA, industry, address(es), phone, website
  • Rating, prequalification status, vendor compliance status
  • Insurance requirements (minimum coverage limits)
  • Tax exempt? (yes/no plus certificate number)
  • Internal notes
📎 Account detail — Documents
Drag-and-drop upload. Files versioned automatically. Expiration dates tracked.
  • Certificates of insurance, W-9, master agreements, vendor onboarding, safety paperwork
  • Each file shows: name, uploaded by, date, expiration
  • Red highlight when expiring within 30 days
👤 Account detail — Contacts
Contacts can have multiple roles. The same person can be Decision Maker AND Billing Contact for one account.
  • Name, email, phone, title
  • Role tags: Decision Maker · PM · Billing · AP · Site contact · Other
  • "Default contact for" lets you set who gets the bid email, who gets the invoice
📊 Account detail — Performance
The lifetime view of this account.
  • Bids submitted, awards, win rate
  • Lifetime revenue, average gross profit %
  • Average payment days (helpful when deciding to bid again)
  • Open A/R total + active projects
2

Opportunities (the bids)

Every bid PPP works on, from "we just got asked" through "we won it" or "we lost it." The pipeline is 12 stages laid out visually so the team always knows where each bid stands.

📋 Pipeline board
Visual columns for each stage. Drag a bid between columns to move it through.
  • Stages: Lead → Qualified → Site Visit → Plans Received → Estimating → Internal Review → Proposal Sent → Follow-up → Negotiation → Verbal Award → Contract Review → Won / Lost / No Bid
  • Each card shows: name, account, value, due date, salesperson
  • Can also flip to a table view for sorting + bulk actions
🎯 Opportunity detail — Info
  • Opportunity name, account, project address
  • Type (new build / repaint / maintenance), estimated value, due date
  • Salesperson
  • Where it came from (email / online form / referral / repeat client)
👥 Opportunity team
The contacts for THIS bid (often different from the account's default contacts — different PM, different decision-maker, etc.).
📐 Plans & Specs
Drag-and-drop the architectural PDFs, specs, addenda, site photos. Big files (20-100MB) supported with upload progress.
📓 Notes & Emails
Free-text notes. Inbound bid emails automatically thread here. Outbound emails from the app go through too — full conversation in one place.

How bids come in

  • Email — Set up a special inbox (like bids@); anything sent there auto-creates an opportunity.
  • Online form — PPP sends a prospect a link, they fill it out, an opp is created.
  • Manual — Estimator types it in (referral, phone call, repeat client).
3

Estimating & Proposals

Where the estimator actually builds the number and crafts the proposal that goes to the client.

✓ Bid qualification checklist
  • Plans received · Specs received · Site visit complete · Scope reviewed · Schedule reviewed · Labor strategy set · Material strategy set
  • Not blocking — yellow badge "5 of 7 done" stays visible at the top
🧮 The estimate workbench
Four collapsible sections; the header shows the running total.
  • Labor — trade/crew, hours, rate, cost
  • Materials — item, quantity, unit cost, markup, cost
  • Equipment — lifts, scaffolds, etc. — rental days, daily rate, cost
  • Subcontractors — sub name, scope, lump sum or unit

Bottom shows: total cost · target margin % · sell price · margin in dollars.

📝 Proposal builder
  • Scope included (bulleted list) · Scope excluded · Assumptions · Addenda · Total $ · Payment terms · Validity period
  • Auto-versioned (Rev 1, Rev 2…) — every save snapshots the prior version so you can diff
  • PDF generated by the app, sent through email with delivery + open tracking
❓ RFIs
Questions back to the client mid-bid. Each one shows: question, when asked, response, when answered. Open more than 5 business days → yellow warning.
🏁 Bid outcome
Three buttons at the top: Awarded · Lost · No Bid / On Hold. Clicking "Awarded" creates a project record automatically. "Lost" asks why (price / competition / timing / scope / other).
4

Contract Award

The brief stop between "we won" and "the project is set up to actually start." Contract value, retainage %, payment terms, plus what needs to be in place before the project can go active.

📃 Contract details
  • Contract value, retainage % (default 5%, override per contract)
  • Payment terms (net 30 / net 45 / progress / other)
  • Signed date + PDF of signed contract
  • Bonding required? + amount
  • Certified payroll required?
🛡️ Pre-construction requirements
Checklist that must be green before the project can become active.
  • Insurance certificates · Bonding · Safety plans · Permits · Certified payroll setup · Other compliance
  • Each row shows status (not started / in progress / complete), who's responsible, due date, doc uploaded
5

Project Setup

The awarded bid becomes the operational project. Everything from this point onward attaches to the project record.

📋 Projects list
All current projects in a card grid. Each card shows project name, contract value, % complete, days remaining, status, owner PM.
🏗️ Project detail — header
Always visible at the top: project name, contract value, status, % complete, days remaining, open punch count.
  • Project name, account, address
  • Contract value (with revised total after change orders)
  • Start date, target completion date
  • Live forecast gross profit $
👥 Project team
Multiple project managers supported — add as many as you need. Different people on different projects. A PM can be on multiple jobs; a super can be assigned to two projects at once.
  • Add team member, pick their role on this project (PM / Super / Foreman / Safety contact)
  • Remove anyone, anytime (except the last PM — must reassign first)
  • Each role can have multiple people (e.g. two co-PMs on a big job)
📑 Project documents
Signed contract · SOW · General conditions · Insurance certs · Permits · Other. All versioned.
📤 Submittals
Product data, samples, finish schedules. Each one flows: Drafted → Submitted → Reviewed → Approved or Rejected (with notes back).
6

Project Execution (day-to-day)

Day-to-day project tracking. This is where the field team lives. Most of these screens are designed for phone-first use.

📱 Daily report (phone-first)
Foreman opens this on their iPhone at the end of the day. Single column, big tap targets, works without signal — syncs when back online.
  • Date, weather, crew on site (multi-select from team)
  • Trades present, hours worked per person
  • Work performed (free text + categories)
  • Production units (sq ft painted, rooms done, etc.)
  • Delays (weather / materials / client / other)
  • Safety incidents (flag)
  • Photos straight from camera
⚒️ Labor management
Budget hours vs actual hours, by crew and by trade. Variance >10% → flag.
🪣 Material management
For each material: budget quantity · purchased · installed · remaining · variance $. Alert when remaining drops below 10%.
🚜 Equipment management
Per-equipment rental tracking. Budget days vs actual. Auto-flag double-booked equipment across projects.
🧱 Subcontractor management
List of subs on the job + their contract values + their invoices.
  • Sub invoice flow: Received → Reviewed → Approved → Paid
  • When a sub invoice arrives, PM gets a notification
  • Invoice exceeds sub contract → warning, requires admin override
🔍 Quality + Inspections
Site inspection log. Findings can become punch items automatically.
✅ Punchlist
Two parallel lists: internal (PPP self-finds) and client (client finds). Each item gets a description, location, assignee, status, photos.

Foreman closes items in the field with a tap + photo proof.

💬 Communications log
Meeting minutes, client emails, phone-call summaries, decisions. Searchable + tagged. Action items become tasks.
📅 Schedule + milestones
Baseline schedule vs current schedule. Milestones (mobilize, frame, paint, punch, complete) with target + actual dates. Slip detection: any milestone more than 7 days late → "delayed" status + notification to PM.
7

Change Orders

Owner-requested changes, field directives, and scope revisions. Each one logged separately so the math always works out at the end.

📋 Change orders list
Per-project board grouped by status: Draft → Submitted → Pending → Approved or Rejected → Invoiced.

Header shows: approved $ total · pending $ total · revised contract total.

📝 Change order detail
  • CO # (auto-numbered per project), type (owner request / field directive / scope revision)
  • Title, description, amount $ (can be negative for deductive COs)
  • Signed CO PDF upload
  • Impact tracking: labor hours, material $, equipment $, schedule days

Approved → automatically adds to revised contract + completion date.

8

Billing & Invoices

This is the most important section for the day-to-day. The workflow PPP wants:

How invoicing works on the platform:
① Multiple invoices per job. When PPP wins a bid and starts working, they invoice as they go. Each invoice covers a specific period (start date + end date) for a specific dollar amount that office staff sets manually.

② Mark payments manually. When a check or wire arrives, office staff opens the invoice and enters how much was paid and when. Full or partial — both work. Multiple partial payments on one invoice are fine.

③ Automatic reminders. If an invoice is past due, or only partially paid after 15 days, the app emails the client a reminder automatically. No one has to remember to chase it.
💵 Project billing tab
All invoices for ONE project in one place.

Top of page: Contract $ · approved change orders $ · billed-to-date $ · paid $ · open balance $ · forecast gross profit $.

Invoice list: filter by All · Paid · Unpaid · Past Due · Draft. Filter by date range too. Each row shows invoice #, period, amount, status, sent date, due date, paid date.

📄 Create a new invoice
Office staff fills this out manually anytime they want to bill.
  • Period start date and end date
  • Amount (set manually for this billing period)
  • Optional: line items (progress, materials, retainage held, approved CO, etc.)
  • Net terms (defaults from contract, override if needed)
  • "Save as Draft" or "Send" — sent invoices email the client a PDF
💳 Record a payment
When money comes in, office staff opens the invoice and enters the payment.
  • Amount paid (can be partial)
  • Date received
  • Payment method (check / ACH / wire)
  • Reference number (check # or wire confirmation)

Multiple payments per invoice supported — the running balance updates automatically. Invoice status flips to "Partially Paid" or "Paid" as the balance hits the total.

⏰ Automatic reminders
No one has to chase invoices manually.
  • Invoice past due by 15 days → automatic email to the client
  • Invoice partially paid with a remaining balance for 15 days → automatic email to the client
  • Office staff gets a notification (bell) too so they know the reminder went out
  • Karan/Alex/PM see a "past due" total at the top of the project header

9Documents & record-keeping

  • Every document uploaded is saved permanently. Plans, specs, contracts, COIs, COs, invoices, photos — all attached to the right account or project.
  • Files are versioned. Replace a COI with a new one — the old version is still there, just archived.
  • Files are kept for 7 years (construction industry standard; longer for warranty docs).
  • Every change is tracked. Who edited what record, when, what was changed. Helpful for audits and for sorting out "wait, who changed that?"
  • Downloads are private. Each download link expires after 1 hour so old emailed links can't be reused.
